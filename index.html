bio

-

Natalia is who she is. A verb more than an adjective, she embodies a Founder-in-Chief, community builder extraordinaire and resident madrina to the many businesses, organizations and humans she helps develop. An organizational psychologist and technologist by training, she is laser focused on merging innovation, entrepreneurship, and impact--this last one being the most important to her. Whether it's business, civic engagement, or the arts (yes, her brain and work often spans across all areas) Natalia's solution to everything is collaboration and creating communities that are truly inclusive ecosystems. This dedication is informed by her life as a twice-immigrant, interest in cross-disciplinary intersections, fascination with complex systems, and desire to bridge opportunity gaps. Currently, she has done so by leading the expansion of the Cambridge Innovation Center to Miami. CIC is the US’ oldest and largest gathering hub of startup activity, labeled a leader in creating innovation districts due to their economic impact, urban development, and community engagement initiatives. Since launching CIC in Miami, Natalia has grown it into a $2M+ business and has overseen various initiatives to deepen and nuance the conversation about entrepreneurship, accelerate Miami's economic development, and build tangible bridges to innovation hubs and Latin America but always with compassion; a must in her values' book.

She is the Founder of Awesome Foundation MIAMI, a micro-grant organization that has awarded close to $100K to small, grassroots ideas in Miami. She is also the Founder of Aminta Ventures, an educational vehicle to empower more female angel investors in South Florida as well as a founding board member of Local TAU, a spin-off platform connecting Tel Aviv University to Miami. Natalia serves as the Vice President of LatAm Expansion for the Latino Startup Alliance, is a member of the World Economic Forum, and has been appointed by Florida Governor Rick Scott to serve on the Governor’s Commission on Community Service, a body that oversees the administration of $24 million in federal, state, and local funding to deliver high-impact educational and volunteer programs in Florida.

When done exercising her left brain, her right brain kicks in. She's the co-creator and curator of "Love Lost, Miami," an annual art exhibit hoping to share a spotlight of the stories of loss through personal and ordinary items that come alive with their stories. A dedicated fan of jazz and the opera, she is also a talented amateur photographer and infuses her own vodka.

Natalia is a regular contributor to outlets such as The Huffington Post, the World Economic Forum, Mic, and La Nacion. She holds a B.A. in Psychology and Government from Harvard University and an M.A. in Organizational Psychology from Columbia University.

A true tour de force, Natalia's body of work is the reason for theNMK.com
