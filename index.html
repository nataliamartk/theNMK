bio

Natalia is who she is. A verb more than a noun, she embodies a Founder-in-Chief, community builder extraordinaire and resident madrina to the many businesses, organizations and humans she helps incubate, develop and thrive. An organizational psychologist and technologist with degrees from Harvard and Columbia University, she is laser focused on merging innovation, entrepreneurship, and lasting impact with heart—the latter being the most important to her. Whether it's business, civic engagement, or the arts (yes, her brain and work often spans across all areas) Natalia's solution to everything is collaboration and creating communities that are truly inclusive ecosystems with aplomb. Just look at what she did with Cambridge Innovation Center’s Miami expansion in under a year. Her dedication is informed by her life as a twice-immigrant née to Russian and Cuban parents, an interdisciplinary approach to life and a fascination with complex systems.

She is the Founder of Awesome Foundation MIAMI, Aminta Ventures as well as a founding Board Member of LocalTAU. Natalia serves as the Vice President of LatAm Expansion for the Latino Startup Alliance, is a member of the World Economic Forum, and was appointed by Florida Governor Rick Scott to serve on the Governor’s Commission on Community Service. When done exercising her left brain, her right brain kicks in. She's the co-creator and curator of "Love Lost, Miami," an annual art exhibit that explores through personal and ordinary items that come alive with their stories. A dedicated fan of jazz and the opera, she is also a talented amateur photographer and infuses her own vodka. Natalia is a cat mom, porteña by choice, a regular speaker and contributor to outlets such as The Huffington Post, the World Economic Forum, Mic, and La Nation. A true tour de force, Natalia's being of work is the reason for theNMK.com
